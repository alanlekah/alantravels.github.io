The Top 5 Best Adults-Only Cruises for 2026

Escape the crowds and enjoy a sophisticated, kid-free vacation at sea.

By Alan Lekah | March 10, 2026

Sometimes you just need to get away from it all—and by "all," we mean the splashing kids, character parades, and high-energy family activities. The demand for adults-only cruises has skyrocketed for 2026, with more travelers looking for sophisticated dining, tranquil pool decks, and nightlife that stays up as late as they do.

As a cruise expert, I’ve vetted the top lines that cater specifically to the 18+ and 21+ crowd. Here are my top 5 picks for an unforgettable kid-free voyage in 2026.

1. Virgin Voyages: The Modern Classic

Virgin Voyages has redefined the adults-only cruise. With "Sailors" required to be 18+, the vibe is more boutique hotel than traditional cruise ship. From drag brunches to late-night DJ sets at The Manor, it’s built for the young-at-heart. Plus, with all specialty dining included, it’s a foodie’s paradise.

2. Viking Ocean Cruises: Pure Sophistication

Viking is the ultimate choice for travelers who value culture and quiet. There are no casinos, no kids under 18 allowed, and no "hard sell" for drinks or excursions. Every ship is a masterpiece of Scandinavian design, and every stateroom features a private veranda.

3. P&O Cruises: Arcadia & Aurora

For those looking for a more traditional British cruising experience, P&O’s Arcadia and Aurora are strictly adults-only. These mid-sized ships offer a more intimate atmosphere, perfect for relaxing sea days and exploring unique ports of call.

4. Saga Cruises: Luxury for the 50+

If you’re over 50, Saga offers a truly all-inclusive luxury experience. From door-to-door chauffeur service to included shore excursions and premium drinks, they handle everything. Their new boutique ships, Spirit of Adventure and Spirit of Discovery, are stunning.

5. Luxury Lines (The "Silent" Adults-Only)

While lines like Regent Seven Seas, Silversea, and Seabourn technically allow children, their price points and lack of kid-focused amenities mean they are effectively adults-only. If you want ultra-luxury where the service is personalized and the crowds are non-existent, these are your best bets.
