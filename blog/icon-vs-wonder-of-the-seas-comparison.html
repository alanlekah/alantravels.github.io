Icon of the Seas vs. Wonder of the Seas: Which Royal Caribbean Giant Wins?

By Alan Lekah | March 10, 2026

Deciding between Royal Caribbean's two biggest ships is like choosing between a 5-star resort and a futuristic theme park. Both Icon of the Seas and Wonder of the Seas offer mind-blowing amenities, but they are built for slightly different types of vacationers. In this guide, I'll break down the neighborhoods, the dining, and the price tags to help you decide which giant is right for your next getaway.

Size and Stats: Who is the Real King?

Until recently, Wonder of the Seas held the crown as the world's largest cruise ship. But Icon of the Seas has officially taken the throne. Icon is roughly 6% larger by gross tonnage and can hold nearly 7,600 guests at max capacity.

  • Icon of the Seas: 250,800 tons, 1,198 feet long.
  • Wonder of the Seas: 236,857 tons, 1,188 feet long.

Neighborhoods: Oasis Class vs. Icon Class

Wonder of the Seas is the pinnacle of the Oasis Class, featuring 8 distinct neighborhoods including the iconic Central Park and the Boardwalk. Icon of the Seas introduces 5 new neighborhoods, including Surfside (specifically for young families) and Thrill Island (home to the largest waterpark at sea).

Why Choose Wonder?

If you love the classic Royal Caribbean feel with the outdoor AquaTheater and the lush greens of Central Park, Wonder is hard to beat. It feels refined and spacious despite its massive size.

Why Choose Icon?

If you want the "Newest and Best," Icon is the clear winner. The AquaDome is a technological marvel that replaces the traditional AquaTheater, and the pool decks on Icon are far more expansive than those on Wonder.

Dining and Entertainment

Both ships feature over 20 dining options. Wonder is famous for The Mason Jar (Southern comfort food), while Icon introduces Empire Supper Club for a high-end jazz-age dining experience. In terms of entertainment, Icon's production of The Wizard of Oz is a major draw, while Wonder's inTENSE all-female aqua show is a fan favorite.

The Verdict: Which One Should You Book?

Choose Wonder of the Seas if: You want a world-class experience at a slightly more accessible price point. Wonder has more availability and often better deals for families on a budget.

Choose Icon of the Seas if: You want the ultimate family vacation and money is less of a concern. The Surfside neighborhood alone makes it the best ship in the world for families with kids under 6.
