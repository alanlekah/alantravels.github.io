Is Virgin Voyages Worth It? A Complete Guide for First-Time Sailors

By Alan Lekah | March 10, 2026

Virgin Voyages has completely disrupted the cruise industry with its "Adults-Only" concept and focus on "Rebellious Luxury." But with a higher-than-average price tag and no traditional buffets or Broadway shows, many travelers find themselves asking: Is Virgin Voyages actually worth it? In this guide, I'll break down the "All-Inclusive" value, the unique onboard vibe, and who this cruise line is (and isn't) for.

What Makes Virgin Voyages Different?

Most cruise lines are built on a "Nickel and Dime" model—you pay a base fare and then pay extra for everything from Wi-Fi to specialty dining. Virgin Voyages takes a different approach. Their "Always Included Luxury" model covers:

  • All 20+ Eateries: No buffets here. Every restaurant, from the steakhouse to the Korean BBQ, is included in your fare.
  • Wi-Fi: Basic Wi-Fi is included for all Sailors.
  • Tips & Gratuities: Virgin has built tips into the base fare, so you don't get a surprise bill at the end.
  • Essential Drinks: Still/sparkling water, sodas, drip coffee, and tea are all free.
  • Group Fitness Classes: From yoga to HIIT, all classes are included.

The "Adults-Only" Vibe

Virgin Voyages is strictly 18+. This means no screaming kids, no character meet-and-greets, and a much more mature atmosphere. The ships (Scarlet Lady, Valiant Lady, Resilient Lady) are designed to feel like boutique hotels or high-end beach clubs rather than traditional cruise ships.

Is it Worth the Premium Price?

When you compare Virgin to a line like Carnival or Royal Caribbean, the "Sticker Price" will look significantly higher. However, once you add up the cost of a drink package, Wi-Fi, and 4 or 5 nights of specialty dining on a traditional ship, Virgin Voyages often ends up being the better value.

The verdict? If you value high-quality food, a sophisticated social scene, and a hassle-free vacation, Virgin is 100% worth it.
